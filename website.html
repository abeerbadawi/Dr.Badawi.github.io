About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at York University and Faculty Affiliate Researcher at Vector Institute, supported by the Connected Minds Scholarship (CFREF). My research focuses on digital biomarkers, machine learning, large language models, and responsible AI with applications in mental health systems.

I earned my PhD in Computer Engineering at Ontario Tech University, where I was nominated for the best thesis award and received the Ontario Graduate Scholarship (OGS). Since 2016, I have been actively engaged in AI and data science research, collaborating with leading organizations including University Health Network, Ontario Shores Hospital, University of Toronto, Vector Institute, and Kids Help Phone.

My work has resulted in 22+ publications in top venues (ICML, IEEE, JMIR) with over 150 citations. I am passionate about leveraging AI to improve healthcare outcomes, particularly in mental health and neuropsychiatric symptom prediction.

Research Areas

Digital Biomarkers

Developing AI-driven approaches to detect and predict neuropsychiatric symptoms using multimodal sensor data and clinical demonstrations.

Large Language Models

Designing ethical and adaptive LLM systems for mental health applications, focusing on bias detection, fairness, and responsible AI implementation.

Mental Health Systems

Creating innovative AI solutions to support mental health diagnosis, treatment, and care delivery through technology-enabled interventions.

Machine Learning

Developing advanced ML algorithms for healthcare applications, including semi-supervised learning and real-time signal processing.
